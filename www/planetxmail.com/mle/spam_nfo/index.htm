Spam

Within the Internet community, 'Spam' refers to the unsolicited commercial mass-mailings that constantly fill up electronic mailboxes and Usenet newsgroups. Spam offers everything from instant riches to a cure for baldness.

Read this, and dramatically decrease the amount of spam you receive.

  • How to decipher email headers. Learn how to read email headers to determine where a message really originated.
  • Who should I complain to? Find out how to complain about spam without adding to the problem.
  • Prevention is the best medicine. Keep your email address off of spam lists. Topics include the safe use of newsgroups, precautions to take when subscribing to online services, and how to be 'spam smart' when replying to surveys and email.
Where Did it Come From?
Internet spam started a few years ago when an Arizona law firm sent advertisements to over 6000 newsgroups. People were outraged and responded by flooding the law firm's ISP with thousands of complaints. Other people posted the lawyer's home addresses and phone numbers, and made death threats against them. The harsh reaction from the net community made others who were considering similar advertising think twice.

There was one man who was undaunted by the potential fallout and saw this as a new business opportunity: Sanford Wallace. Wallace began the company 'Cyberpromo', a company that would gain infamy around the Internet as one of the worst offenders of spamming. This company has appeared many times in the newspaper and on television as the "King of Spam".
