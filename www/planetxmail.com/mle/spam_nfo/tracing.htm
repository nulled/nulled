Tracing Email

Once you know the domain names and IP addresses, you can begin tracing the message's origin. The goal is to verify that the domain names are correct, according to the given addresses. Domain Name Systems (DNS) servers let you enter look up information on specific IP addresses. You can compare this information with the information in your email header to find the origin of a message. People may change the domain name in the email header, but the address is always correct. To look up the information on an IP address do the following:
  1. Go to the traceroute tools.
  2. Enter the IP address in the search box and click search.
  3. Compare the name of the domain (computer) with each IP address in your email.
Machine Name IP Address Match?
mail.compuserve.com 205.5.81.86 No
hd58-032.hil.compuserve.com 199.174.238.32 Yes
pacific.net.sg 203.120.90.85 Yes
pacific.net.sg 203.120.90.72 Yes

In this example, the first server and IP address do not match. The IP address was 'invented' by someone to keep others from finding out who sent the message. However, the next address is correct, and was probably the sender's own address. The 'hd58-032' on a compuserve address is common for a dial-up account, rather than a specific machine on a network.

The next machine, pacific.net.sg., is a remote post office (a remailer). The spammer sent the spam through a remote post office server, which sent it to the receiver. The spammer also tried to cover the tracks by adding a fake entry to the mail header.

Now you know who to contact so action can be taken against the spammer. In this case, it would be CompuServe.
